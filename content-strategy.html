Phase 3

Content Strategy: Anjie & RJ Muhammad

Overview

The Rich by Intention content engine is already running. The gap is not content volume - it is conversion architecture. The existing podcast, book, and IG presence build enough trust to fill a cohort. The content strategy below layers conversion-intent content on top of the existing publishing cadence without requiring a production overhaul.

Content Pillars

Pillar 1 — Social Proof as Storytelling

Document real couples (with permission) who have used the RBI framework: their before-numbers, their turning-point moment, their current status. Short-form video works well. These are not testimonials - they are companion stories. They naturalise the cohort as something real couples join, not a course couples buy.

Pillar 2 — Couple-Conflict Content

Content that names the exact money arguments couples have (who spends more, whose income "counts more," whether the emergency fund takes priority over the vacation). RBI's trust comes from naming real marital friction. Content that speaks to the specific emotional texture of couples managing money together will outperform generic budgeting content every time.

Pillar 3 — "The Math Made Simple" Series

Short-form visual explainers: what debt payoff actually costs in time at different income levels. A couple earning $90K combined with $80K in debt can be debt-free in 36 months if they install the right system. Make the math visual, make it specific to a recognisable income band, and show the timeline. This is the hook that routes listeners to the Couples Debt Payoff Timeline Calculator.

Pillar 4 — Cohort Behind-the-Scenes

When the Couples Wealth Mentorship launches, document the experience: the calls, the accountability pod check-ins, the week 8 debt-payoff milestones. This content sells the next cohort while fulfilling the current one.
