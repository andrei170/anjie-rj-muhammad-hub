Foundation

Copy Strategy: Anjie & RJ Muhammad

Voice Profile

Source voice: Anjie and RJ Muhammad, Rich by Intention

Register: Warm, peer-to-peer, faith-adjacent. They speak to their audience as fellow travellers who got ahead on the journey, not as gurus looking down. The copy must honour both voices - Anjie's warmth and RJ's directness. Neither is the "main" voice. They are a team.

Tone characteristics: - Plain spoken, no jargon - Faith language used naturally, not performatively - Numbers cited specifically (actual debt amounts, actual timelines) not vaguely - Community-first framing: "we did this together" not "I did this for you" - Warmth is earned through specificity, not through exclamation points

Brand Pillars

  1. Intention over accident. Financial success for couples is not luck. It is a system applied with shared intention. Copy positions the Muhammads as proof that intention scales.
  2. Marriage as the unit. All copy speaks to the couple together, not to one partner. The offer is for couples. The problem is a couples problem. The solution is built for two.
  3. Public proof. Six-figure debt paid off publicly. This is the foundation credibility claim. Copy can and should reference it without over-explaining it.
  4. Award-winning is earned. The podcast's award status signals community trust. Do not oversell it. Let it do quiet work.
  5. Stewardship, not hustle. The Rich by Intention worldview is not "get rich" framing. It is "manage what you have been given with intention." Copy must respect this distinction.

Headline Bank

For landing page hero: - "The Couples Wealth Mentorship. For couples who are done doing this alone." - "12 weeks. Two of you. One debt-free timeline." - "You have done the podcasts. You have read the books. This is what accountability actually looks like." - "If you want us in the room with you, not just in your earbuds." - "The Rich by Intention playbook, installed with you in it."

For email subject lines: - "Your debt-free date is closer than you think" - "What 30 couples did in 12 weeks" - "The accountability piece most debt plans skip" - "For couples serious about the timeline" - "Three couples. Three different debt amounts. One thing in common."

For IG hooks: - "We paid off six figures. You already know that. Here is what we built so you could do it too." - "The course teaches the system. The cohort installs it with you in it." - "If your spouse is not bought in, everything else is harder."

Copy Rules for All RBI-Voice Content

  1. Both names in every header. "Anjie and RJ" not "the Muhammads" not "they." Address the pair directly.
  2. Couples-plural language. "You two" not "you." "Your household" not "your income." "Together" is used deliberately, not as filler.
  3. Faith language is natural, not decorative. Do not force faith references. When they appear, they should feel like something Anjie or RJ would say, not like it was inserted for the market segment.
  4. Specificity over aspiration. "$70K paid off in 28 months" lands better than "transform your financial life." Always reach for the specific number over the abstract promise.
  5. The course stays respected. Never position the Couples Wealth Mentorship as "better than" the self-paced course. They serve different buyers. The mentorship is for the couple who wants accountability alongside the system.
  6. Zero em dashes. Use commas, periods, or line breaks.
  7. No AI slop. Banned: game-changer, transform, unlock, abundance, manifest, journey, quantum leap, supercharge, revolutionary.

Framing for the AG1 Outreach

When writing as Andrei about Anjie and RJ: - Lead with what is observable: the podcast, the book, the courses, the 26K audience - Frame the gap clinically: the offer tier that converts trust into recurring revenue does not exist yet - Never frame their existing offers as failures: they are the foundation, not the problem - Frame the mentorship as the next natural layer: the audience is ready, the product is not

Tone for outreach to this prospect: Collegiate, warm, slightly informal. This is a faith-aligned married couple who run a community brand. Match the register of someone who respects both what they have built and what is missing. Not a cold pitch. A peer observation.
