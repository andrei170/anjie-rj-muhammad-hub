Research File: Anjie & RJ Muhammad

1. Identity

Names: Anjie Muhammad and RJ Muhammad (husband-wife team) Brand: Rich by Intention Instagram: @richbyintention (26K followers) Website: richbyintention.com Email: richbyintention@gmail.com Niche: Couples wealth coaching - debt elimination, financial intentionality, faith-aligned financial freedom

Public profile: Anjie and RJ Muhammad are a married couple who publicly paid off six figures of debt and built a financial education brand around that journey. They run the Rich by Intention podcast (award-winning), have authored a book, and sell self-paced financial courses. Their brand sits at the intersection of personal finance, faith, and marriage - targeting couples who want to align their money values before and during debt payoff.

2. Audience

Primary audience: Married couples (or engaged couples) aged 28-42, faith-motivated, carrying $40K-$150K in combined debt (student loans, credit cards, consumer debt). The audience is not broke - they have income, two earners, and the capacity to pay debt fast if they have a system and accountability. They follow RBI because the Muhammads "look like them" - Black Christian couples who speak plainly about money without shame.

Audience size: 26K IG + podcast listenership (award-winning shows typically 5K-50K consistent downloads per episode) + email list via richbyintention.com opt-ins + book buyers.

Engagement signal: Award-winning podcast = active, loyal listenership. Podcast listeners buy at higher rates than social followers. This is a warm, trust-dense audience.

Audience psychology: - Exhausted by debt but not financially illiterate - they know what to do, they lack accountability and execution support - Marriage-money tension is real: different spending personalities, conflicting financial priorities - Faith lens is important: they want stewardship framing, not just hustle framing - They want proof the Muhammads did it, which they have (six-figure public debt payoff) - Community matters: they do not want to feel alone in the journey

3. Current Offer Stack

Offer Format Price est. Notes
Rich by Intention Podcast Free, award-winning $0 Primary audience builder
RBI Book Physical/digital $15-25 Likely Amazon + own store
Self-paced courses Digital course $97-297 est. Budgeting, debt payoff frameworks

What's missing: No high-touch, live, accountable offer. No cohort. No small-group mentorship. The audience pays $15-25 for the book, maybe $197 for a course - and then is on their own. There is no offer for the couple that wants Anjie and RJ in the room with them.

4. Kabrin Tier Classification

Tier: #1 Early-Stage Operator

Why: 26K IG + award-winning podcast = proof of audience quality and loyalty. Book + course catalog = proof of offer-building competency. Zero productised mentorship = the gap is the opportunity. This is a classic skilled-with-audience, no-high-ticket configuration. Every piece is in place except the offer that converts the audience's trust into recurring cohort revenue.

5. Public Proof Inventory

  • Award-winning podcast: documented, publicly searchable
  • Six-figure debt payoff: publicly discussed as the brand origin story
  • Book published (tangible credibility asset)
  • Course catalog (proof of digital product experience)
  • 26K IG audience (organic, not bought - faith-adjacent audiences build slowly)

6. Funnel State

What exists: Organic top-of-funnel (podcast + IG) -> light mid-funnel (book/course sales) -> nothing below that. No cohort. No application page. No live coaching container.

What's missing: A productised mentorship offer with a landing page, a clear CTA from the podcast, and an email nurture sequence that moves warm listeners from "I love this podcast" to "I need to be in that cohort."

Biggest bottleneck: The gap between the warm, award-winning podcast audience and a productised $2K-$4K couples mentorship cohort that does not exist yet. Every listener who finishes an episode and wants more has nowhere to go except a self-paced course. The self-paced course does not solve their core problem: accountability as a couple.

7. Sensitivity Profile

  • Faith is central, not decorative: any pitch must respect the stewardship framing, not secularise it
  • This is a couples brand - any outreach addresses both Anjie and RJ, never just one
  • The debt payoff story is the foundation of their credibility - never diminish it or treat it as old news
  • They are community builders, not gurus: peer-to-peer register lands better than authority-from-above framing
  • Courses are NOT the enemy: their existing courses stay live. The mentorship sits alongside as a higher-tier option, never as a replacement

8. Pitch Leverage

Why they say yes: - The audience is already there. The cohort does not require building an audience - it requires giving the existing audience somewhere to go. - The podcast produces warm, high-trust leads every week. A Couples Wealth Mentorship cohort converts those leads directly. - The math is immediate: 20-30 couples at $2K-$4K = $40-120K on the first cohort. Repeatable quarterly. - They have done the hard part (built the audience, built the credibility). The missing piece is the funnel architecture around the cohort.
